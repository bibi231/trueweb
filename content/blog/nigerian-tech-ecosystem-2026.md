## Taking Stock of Where We Are

Nigeria's tech ecosystem has had a turbulent few years. The 2022–2023 period saw layoffs, down-rounds, and a recalibration of valuations across African tech. What emerged from that period is a leaner, more realistic ecosystem — one less focused on growth-at-all-costs and more attentive to unit economics and real market fit.

In 2026, the companies that are scaling are those that solved genuine Nigerian problems and built business models that work at Nigerian price points.

## The Sectors That Have Matured

### Payments and Fintech
This is the most developed layer of the ecosystem. Paystack (Stripe-acquired), Flutterwave, Moniepoint, OPay, PalmPay, and a dozen others have built payment infrastructure that now works reliably at scale. The innovation frontier has shifted from basic payment rails to credit, insurance, and embedded finance.

The remaining gap: SMB financial services. Most Nigerian businesses outside Lagos and Abuja still have inadequate access to working capital, trade finance, and business banking products built for their operating realities.

### Logistics
Jumia Logistics, Kobo360, Sendbox, and GIG Logistics have built infrastructure for e-commerce fulfilment. The open market remains last-mile delivery for non-e-commerce industries — FMCG distributors, manufacturers, and healthcare supply chains.

### Edtech
Andela's shift from a talent platform to a pure marketplace reflected broader edtech consolidation. In 2026, the focus has moved from consumer edtech (which consistently struggled with willingness to pay) to B2B training and professional upskilling, where corporate procurement provides more stable revenue.

## The Sectors Gaining Momentum

### Healthtech
Electronic health records, telemedicine, pharmacy management, and health insurance technology are all attracting founder attention. The public health system's digitalisation push post-COVID created both urgency and procurement pathways. LifeBank, Helium Health, and newer entrants are building infrastructure that the healthcare sector badly needs.

### AgriTech
Nigeria's agricultural modernisation is slow but moving. Beyond input provision (which Agrorite, Thrive Agric, and others have attacked), the opportunity in commodity trading platforms, produce aggregation, and traceability is significant and largely uncaptured.

### ClimaTech and Energy
Load-shedding and grid unreliability have created a large market for distributed energy solutions. Solar-as-a-service models are expanding, and the software layer — energy management, metering, and customer billing for mini-grids — is a growing vertical.

## What the Next Wave Looks Like

Several structural shifts are shaping the companies being built now:

**AI-native products**: Nigerian founders are building AI tools for specific professional contexts — legal document drafting in Nigerian law, Yoruba/Igbo/Hausa NLP, automated accounting reconciliation for Nigerian tax formats. Global AI tools serve broad markets; local AI serves specific compliance and language requirements.

**B2B infrastructure**: The consumer wave is slowing. The next value is in selling to businesses — software for distributors, manufacturers, logistics operators, healthcare providers, and professional services firms.

**Diaspora capital and talent**: Nigeria's tech diaspora is investing and building at home more than at any previous point. This brings both capital access and technical talent that previously stayed offshore.

## What Has Not Changed

The structural constraints remain: power costs, foreign exchange access, regulatory uncertainty, and the challenge of building for a market where ability and willingness to pay diverge. The founders who navigate this well build with these constraints as design inputs, not as external problems to solve after product-market fit.

Nigeria's tech ecosystem is not going to be Silicon Valley. It's going to be something different — built for its own market, eventually exporting to other African markets, and producing category-defining companies in sectors that global tech barely thinks about.
