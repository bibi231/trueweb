## Why Prices Vary So Widely

Website quotes in Nigeria range from ₦50,000 to ₦15,000,000 for what might seem like similar projects. That range is real and reflects genuine differences in what you are buying — not just agency margin variation.

Understanding what drives cost helps you evaluate quotes intelligently and avoid buying the wrong tier for your needs.

## Tier 1: Template Sites (₦80,000 – ₦350,000)

**What this gets you:**
- A professionally customised WordPress or Webflow template
- Your content, logo, and colours applied
- Mobile responsive, basic contact form
- Launched in 1–2 weeks

**What it doesn't include:**
- Custom design (the layout is pre-built)
- Custom functionality beyond standard plugins
- SEO optimisation beyond basic on-page setup
- Ongoing updates

**Right for**: Service businesses (salons, clinics, restaurants, consultants) that need a credible online presence and don't have complex requirements.

## Tier 2: Custom Business Websites (₦500,000 – ₦2,500,000)

**What this gets you:**
- Custom UI/UX design specific to your brand and user flow
- Multi-page site with considered information architecture
- CMS integration so non-technical staff can update content
- Performance-optimised build (Core Web Vitals compliant)
- Basic SEO architecture built in
- 4–8 weeks delivery timeline

**What it doesn't include:**
- Backend databases or user accounts
- Payments, bookings, or complex integrations (these add cost)

**Right for**: SMEs and growing businesses that want a competitive digital presence — professional services firms, B2B companies, retail brands.

## Tier 3: Web Applications and SaaS Platforms (₦3,000,000 – ₦15,000,000+)

**What this gets you:**
- Custom application development (not just a website — software)
- User authentication, database design, API integrations
- Admin dashboards, reporting, workflow automation
- Multi-role access control
- Scalable infrastructure setup
- 8–24 week timeline depending on scope

**Right for**: Companies building a product (SaaS, marketplace, client portal) or needing a website with significant functional requirements (online ordering, booking systems, member portals).

## What Pushes Costs Up

- **Custom integrations** (payment gateways, CRMs, accounting software): each adds ₦150,000–₦500,000+
- **E-commerce functionality**: product catalogue, cart, checkout, and inventory management roughly doubles the base cost
- **Multilingual or multi-currency support** adds complexity
- **Security and compliance requirements** (fintech, healthcare) require additional engineering
- **Urgent timelines**: rush work at 60–70% of normal scope does not produce 60% of the quality

## Recurring Costs to Budget For

A website is not a one-time purchase. Ongoing costs include:

| Item | Annual Cost Range |
|------|------------------|
| Domain (.com.ng) | ₦5,000 – ₦15,000 |
| Hosting | ₦30,000 – ₦300,000 |
| SSL certificate | Free (Let's Encrypt) – ₦50,000 |
| Maintenance retainer | ₦50,000 – ₦200,000/month |
| Content updates | Variable |

## How to Read a Quote

A quote that seems unusually low is either cutting corners on design quality, using outdated technology, outsourcing to a less experienced subcontractor, or omitting items you'll discover later as extras.

Ask every vendor:
- What technology stack are you building on?
- Who is doing the design and development work?
- What does ongoing support cost after launch?
- What happens if we need changes after handover?

Price is one input. Delivered quality and post-launch reliability are the ones that matter for your business.
