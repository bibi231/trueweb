## The Question Most Business Owners Get Wrong

Nigerian business owners often frame this as "build vs buy" and approach it as a cost question. Custom is expensive; off-the-shelf is cheaper. That framing misses the real issue.

The right question is: **does an off-the-shelf solution exist that fits your workflow well enough to be worth the compromises?** If yes, buy. If no, build. Cost is a secondary consideration.

## When Off-the-Shelf Wins

For the vast majority of business functions, existing software products are the better choice:

- **Accounting and invoicing**: Zoho Books, QuickBooks, or Wave handle Nigerian VAT rules and support NGN. Building an accounting module is expensive and the domain complexity is underestimated.
- **Email marketing**: Mailchimp, Brevo, and similar tools are robust and affordable. There is no good reason to build this.
- **CRM**: HubSpot's free tier, Zoho CRM, or Pipedrive serve most Nigerian SMB sales processes well.
- **HR and payroll**: Bento Africa, Workpay, and Seamfix are purpose-built for Nigerian payroll, pensions, and statutory deductions.

Off-the-shelf wins when: the process it handles is generic across industries, the product has been built and maintained by a team with deep domain expertise, and your differentiation as a business does not depend on how you execute that function.

## When Custom Software Makes Sense

Build custom when:

### 1. Your Core Process Is Your Competitive Advantage
If the way you operate is genuinely different from competitors and that difference is source of your business value, off-the-shelf tools will constrain it. A logistics company with a proprietary route optimisation methodology cannot fully express it in a generic TMS.

### 2. No Existing Solution Fits the Nigerian Context
Global tools sometimes fail on local requirements — Naira-denominated multi-wallet accounting, IPPIS payroll compliance, or workflows that depend on WhatsApp-based communication chains. When adaptation costs are high and the result is still compromised, custom is justified.

### 3. You're Building a Product, Not Running a Function
If the software is something you will sell to customers or license to partners — it's a product, and it should be custom. SaaS built on off-the-shelf white-labelling rarely produces durable competitive moats.

### 4. Integration Requirements Are Complex
If you need to integrate 5–8 systems that don't have native connectors and the data transformation between them is significant, a custom middleware layer or application may be cheaper than the integration platform costs and maintenance.

## The Honest Cost Comparison

| Dimension | Off-the-Shelf | Custom |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| Upfront cost | Low to moderate | High |
| Time to deploy | Days to weeks | Weeks to months |
| Ongoing cost | Subscription fees | Maintenance + hosting |
| Fit to your process | Compromise | Built to spec |
| Dependency risk | Vendor continuity | Your team/vendor |
| Scalability | Vendor's roadmap | Your control |

## A Middle Path: Configuration Before Customisation

Before committing to custom development, exhaust configuration options in off-the-shelf tools. Modern SaaS products have extensive customisation through:

- Custom fields and objects
- Workflow automation (Zapier, Make)
- API integrations with bespoke scripts

Many Nigerian businesses that think they need custom software actually need a well-configured Zoho or HubSpot instance with some custom integrations — at a fraction of the build cost.

Build custom only after you've verified that available tools genuinely cannot meet your requirements.
