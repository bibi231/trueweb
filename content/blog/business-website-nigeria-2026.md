## The Wrong Things Get All the Attention

When Nigerian business owners think about a website, the conversation usually starts with "how it looks." The colour scheme, the logo placement, the fonts. These matter — but they're rarely the reason a website fails to deliver business value.

What actually determines whether a website works:

- Does it load fast enough on mobile data connections?
- Does a first-time visitor immediately understand what you do and who it's for?
- Does it make the next step (call, form, purchase) frictionless?
- Does it rank for the searches your customers are running?

A beautiful website that takes 11 seconds to load on a 4G connection in Ibadan is not a business asset. It's a liability.

## What Has Changed Heading Into 2026

### Core Web Vitals Are Now Table Stakes

Google's page experience signals — loading speed (LCP), interactivity (INP), and visual stability (CLS) — directly affect search rankings. Nigerian businesses that ignored technical performance two years ago are now paying for it in organic visibility.

### AI-Generated Content Has Raised the Bar

Because every sector is now flooded with generic AI-written content, **specificity and credibility** signal are more valuable. Case studies, team bios, client logos, and demonstrated sector knowledge distinguish real businesses from content farms.

### WhatsApp Integration Is Expected

For most Nigerian business audiences, WhatsApp is the preferred contact channel. A website without a visible WhatsApp button in 2026 creates unnecessary friction.

## The Foundations That Actually Matter

### 1. Mobile Performance
Over 75% of Nigerian web traffic comes from mobile devices — many on 4G with variable speeds. Your website should score above 80 on Google PageSpeed for mobile. If it doesn't, you're losing a significant share of every visitor.

### 2. Clear Value Proposition
Within three seconds of landing, a visitor should be able to answer: what does this company do, who is it for, and why should I trust them? If your homepage can't answer these, redesign it before adding any other features.

### 3. Trust Signals
Nigerian buyers are cautious online. Include:
- Physical address (not just a contact form)
- Phone number that actually connects
- Client logos or case studies
- CAC registration number for B2B businesses

### 4. SEO Architecture
The way your pages are structured, titled, and linked determines whether Google can understand and rank your content. This is a technical decision, not a design one — and it should be considered from day one, not retrofitted later.

### 5. Reliable Hosting
Shared hosting that goes down during peak hours costs you business. In 2026, managed hosting on platforms like Vercel, Netlify, or Render is accessible and affordable. There is no good reason to be on shared hosting with regular downtime.

A website that performs well, communicates clearly, and builds trust will outlast any that merely looks impressive in a mockup.
