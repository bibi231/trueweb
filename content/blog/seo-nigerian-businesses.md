## Why SEO Is Different for Nigerian Businesses

Nigerian search behaviour has specific patterns that global SEO guides don't account for:

- Many searches include geographic qualifiers: "lawyer Abuja", "cold room supplier Lagos", "generator repair Kano"
- Nigerian users frequently search brand terms with competitors' names to compare
- Mobile-first search means voice and conversational queries are significant
- Google.com.ng and Google.com both serve Nigerian users; the SERPs can differ

Understanding these patterns changes your keyword strategy and content approach.

## Keyword Research for Nigerian Audiences

### Start with Intent, Not Volume

A keyword like "business registration" gets high global search volume but most of that is outside Nigeria. The relevant keyword is "company registration Nigeria" or "CAC registration Lagos" — lower volume but directly from your target audience.

Tools to use:
- **Google Search Console** — shows what queries are already bringing people to your site
- **Google Keyword Planner** with Nigeria as the target location
- **AnswerThePublic** for question-format keywords your audience asks
- **Google's autocomplete and Related Searches** — free, and reflects actual Nigerian query patterns

### Long-Tail Keywords Win at SMB Budget

Nigerian SMBs cannot outbid Jumia or Dangote on broad terms. Target specific, lower-competition long-tail queries:
- "how to export cashew nuts from Nigeria"
- "HR software for Nigerian SMEs"
- "import duty calculator Nigeria 2026"

These convert better anyway — a visitor searching that specifically knows what they want.

## Local SEO: Google Business Profile

For any Nigerian business serving a local audience (not just digital customers), Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is the highest-ROI SEO investment:

1. **Claim and verify your listing** at business.google.com
2. **Complete every field**: hours, description, category, photos, services
3. **Collect reviews** — consistently asking satisfied customers to leave Google reviews compounds over time
4. **Post updates** — new services, events, offers signal an active listing
5. **Respond to all reviews** — including negative ones, professionally

For queries like "accounting firm Lekki" or "printing press Ikeja", the local pack (map results) gets more clicks than organic results.

## Technical SEO Foundations

These are table stakes — get them right before anything else:

- **HTTPS**: Every page must be served over HTTPS. Non-HTTPS sites get ranking penalties and browser warnings.
- **Mobile performance**: Score above 80 on Google PageSpeed for mobile (test at pagespeed.web.dev)
- **Correct page titles and meta descriptions**: Each page needs a unique, descriptive title tag (50–60 characters)
- **Structured data**: Schema markup for businesses, articles, FAQs, and events helps Google understand your content
- **Sitemap**: Submit an XML sitemap in Google Search Console

## Content That Ranks for Nigerian Audiences

Content wins when it:
- Answers a real question Nigerian businesses or consumers ask
- Is more thorough and accurate than what currently ranks
- Demonstrates genuine expertise and local knowledge

The Nigerian context matters: if you're writing about import procedures, reference NAFDAC or NCS. If you're writing about employment, reference Nigerian Labour Act provisions. Generic global content with "Nigeria" inserted does not fool Google or readers.

## Building Links in Nigeria

Backlinks from credible Nigerian sources carry significant weight:

- **Get listed** in relevant directories: LCCI, NACCIMA member directories, sector association websites
- **Contribute to BusinessDay, TechCabal, Nairametrics** — guest columns or data contributions
- **Partner pages** with complementary businesses
- **Local press coverage** — a feature in a regional publication with a website link moves the needle

SEO in Nigeria is less competitive than most mature markets, which means consistent technical and content fundamentals produce visible results faster.
